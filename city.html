Marrakech: The Essentials

An anthology of the best things to do in Marrakech

Nestled in the sumptuous Atlas Mountains, the Moroccan capital of culture, land of Islamic art, is full of historic sites bathed in sunshine and Berber tradition. Here is a list of the best tourist attractions for a journey of the senses in the heart of Marrakech.

1.The museum of Marrakech

A former Mnebbi palace, The Marrakech Museum organizes exhibitions of Moroccan art from antiquity to the present day. There are also historical works, coins and pottery from the region.

2.Jemaa el-Fna square

On the large historic market in Jemaa el-Fna square, barter is the rule and allowspeople to acquire souvenirs, local specialties and clothes. Nearby,you can venture into the medina (old town) and its labyrinth of traditional souks.

3.Menara

With the Atlas as a spectacular backdrop, the Ménara, a true haven of peace, is very popular with locals and visitors eager to escape the din of the city.

4.Bahia Palace

The word "Bahia" means "brilliant". Built for a former slave who had become the sultan's favorite, the Bahia Palace had the ambition to be the most sumptuous of its time!

Marrakech: organize your visit

Where to stay?

Marrakech owes much of its charm to riads, traditional Moroccan residences with private patios.
  The most beautiful of them have now been transformed into hotels and it is advisable to locate them in search
  a unique place to stay. Riad Dar Rbaa Laroub, Riad Edward and Dar Hanane, one of the oldest residences
  of the medina, are generally the most affordable.

Choose your outfit

Although believers, most Moroccans are tolerant. Pay attention to how people around
you are dressed because some parts of the city are more conservative than others. For the fairer sex
in particular, too light an outfit can be a pretext for harassment. It is also best to
inform in advance of the dress code of the places where you go, especially if you visit
a religious site or a monument of the city.

What budget to plan?

The budget to be provided depends on the nature of your stay. However, count in the 300 to 500 MAD per day for
 meals and around 50 MAD for the taxi. A taxi ride from or to the airport should
 cost 200 MAD and buying souvenirs is usually a small part of the budget. To spend the
less possible, do not hesitate to negotiate the prices and be sure to always pay in the local currency (Moroccan dirham).
 That said, having a few euros, pounds or US dollars with you can be useful in an emergency.
